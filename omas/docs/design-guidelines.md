# Design Guidelines

This document provides comprehensive visual design guidelines for Omas Coffee, including photography, voice & tone, iconography, and secondary design elements.

## Quick Start
Reference this guide when creating marketing materials, taking photos, writing copy, or designing any branded content. This ensures consistent creative expression across all touchpoints.

---

## Photography & Imagery Style

### Product Photography
- **Lighting:** Natural light or warm artificial lighting, golden hour aesthetic
- **Backgrounds:** Dark walnut wood surfaces, cream linen, or deep black
- **Angles:** 45-degree overhead for drinks, straight-on for packaging
- **Styling:** Minimal props, focus on product, occasional butterfly or oak leaf accent
- **Treatment:** Slightly desaturated with warm tones, matte finish feel

### Lifestyle Imagery
- **Setting:** Cozy, intimate spaces with warm lighting
- **Mood:** Afternoon relaxation, quiet moments, connection
- **People:** Diverse ages, focus on hands holding cups, close conversations
- **Environment:** Natural textures (wood, linen, ceramics), plants, soft focus backgrounds
- **Time of Day:** Golden hour lighting, afternoon sun through windows

### Social Media Content
- **Grid Aesthetic:** Cohesive warm tones, alternating product and lifestyle shots
- **Filters:** Consistent warm filter, slight grain for nostalgia
- **Composition:** Rule of thirds, negative space for text overlays
- **Behind-the-Scenes:** Cart setup, brewing process, ingredient sourcing

### Image Treatments
- **Overlay:** Subtle wood grain texture at 10-20% opacity on dark backgrounds
- **Vignette:** Light darkening at edges for focus (20-30% edge burn)
- **Color Grading:** Boost warm tones (golds +15%, browns +10%), reduce cool tones (-20% blues)
- **Contrast:** Medium-high for premium feel (+10-15% contrast, -5% highlights)

### Photography Technical Specifications

#### Camera Settings (Recommended)
- **Aperture:** f/2.8-f/5.6 for product shots (soft background blur)
- **Shutter Speed:** 1/60 minimum handheld, 1/125 for movement
- **ISO:** Keep below 800 for minimal grain (intentional grain in post)
- **White Balance:** 5000-5500K (warm daylight), adjust warmer in post

#### Equipment Suggestions
- **Camera:** DSLR or mirrorless with manual controls
- **Lens:** 50mm or 85mm prime for portraits/products, 24-70mm for versatility
- **Lighting:** Natural window light preferred, reflectors for fill
- **Props:** Weathered wood surfaces, linen napkins, vintage spoons, fresh herbs

#### Composition Guidelines
- **Rule of Thirds:** Place focal points at intersection points
- **Negative Space:** 30-40% minimum for text overlay capability
- **Angles:** Eye-level for connection, 45° for appetizing overhead shots
- **Depth:** Layer foreground, subject, background for dimension

## Voice & Tone Guidelines

### Brand Voice
- **Warm & Personal:** Like a conversation with a beloved grandmother
- **Knowledgeable:** Confident in coffee craft without being pretentious
- **Inviting:** Welcoming newcomers while honoring tradition
- **Nostalgic:** References to memory and tradition without living in the past

### Tone Attributes
- **Primary:** Warm, welcoming, genuine, crafted
- **Avoid:** Corporate, rushed, trendy, overly casual

### Key Phrases
- "Take a moment"
- "Crafted with care"
- "A tradition reimagined"
- "Time well spent"
- "Where memories meet"
- "Afternoon ritual"
- "Gemütlichkeit in a cup"

### German Language Usage
- **When to Use:** Tagline, menu items, occasional social media posts
- **How to Use:** Always provide English translation or context
- **Key Terms:**
  - Kaffeezeit (coffee time)
  - Gemütlichkeit (cozy warmth)
  - Kaffeekränzchen (coffee gathering)
  - Hausgemacht (homemade)
  - Mit Liebe (with love)

### Writing Style
- Short, meaningful sentences for impact
- Focus on experience and emotion over technical details
- Use storytelling when describing origin or process
- Personal pronouns (we, our) to create connection

### Brand Voice Examples

#### Welcome Message
"Welcome to our little piece of home. Every cup we brew carries the warmth of Oma's kitchen and the tradition of German Kaffeezeit. Take a moment—you're family here."

#### Product Description (Cappuccino)
"Our signature cappuccino marries robust espresso with velvety steamed milk, topped with a delicate foam leaf. Served in a warm cup that fits perfectly between your palms—just as Oma intended."

#### Social Media Caption
"That golden afternoon pause ☕ When the clock strikes three and the world slows down just enough to savor. What memories are you brewing today? #Kaffeezeit #OmasCoffee"

#### Error Message (Sold Out)
"Oh dear, we've shared our last [item] for today! Like the best family gatherings, some things are worth waiting for. Try our [alternative] or visit us tomorrow for a fresh batch."

#### Email Sign-off
"Until our next Kaffeezeit,
The Omas Coffee Family"

#### Seasonal Announcement
"As autumn's golden leaves mirror our butterfly's wings, we're warming hearts with our new Spiced Apple Cake—Oma's recipe, your new tradition. Limited time, unlimited memories."

## Iconography System

### Icon Style
- **Type:** Line icons with 2-3px stroke weight
- **Color:** Antique gold (#d4af37) primary, cream (#faf8f3) for dark backgrounds
- **Complexity:** Simple, recognizable, not overly detailed
- **Style:** Slightly rounded corners, elegant proportions

### Core Icons Needed
- Coffee cup (from above, steam optional)
- Coffee bean (single, stylized)
- Location pin (for cart location)
- Clock (for hours)
- Heart (for favorites/loyalty)
- Leaf (oak leaf style, for German heritage)
- Butterfly (simplified version of motif)

### Icon Usage
- Always paired with text labels for clarity
- Consistent size relationships (icons slightly smaller than text)
- Adequate spacing around icons (minimum 8px)
- Use sparingly to avoid clutter

### Relationship to Butterfly Motif
- Butterfly icon should be most detailed/ornate
- Other icons simplified to not compete
- Can use butterfly as decorative element, other icons as functional

## Secondary Design Elements

### Border Styles
- **Single Line:** 1-2px antique gold for subtle separation
- **Double Line:** Two parallel 1px lines, 4px apart (German guild style)
- **Ornamental:** Thin line with small butterfly or oak leaf at center
- **Corner Accents:** Small ornamental corners (L-shaped flourishes)

### Ornamental Dividers
- **Centered:** Small butterfly or oak leaf icon between sections
- **Line with Icon:** Horizontal antique gold line, icon at center breaking line
- **Dotted:** Series of small dots (antique gold) for subtle separation
- **Flourish:** Traditional German ornamental scrollwork (use sparingly)

### Pattern Usage

#### Wood Grain Texture
- **Application:** Subtle overlay on dark backgrounds (10-20% opacity)
- **Scale:** Natural scale, not too large or small
- **Color:** Walnut tones matching brand palette
- **Use Cases:** Website backgrounds, packaging interiors, print materials

#### Geometric Pattern (Optional)
- **Style:** Subtle hexagon or diamond grid
- **Color:** Antique gold at 5-10% opacity
- **Scale:** Small, 20-30px repeat
- **Use:** Accent areas, background for text sections

### Background Treatments

#### Gradient Overlays
- **Direction:** Subtle vertical or radial
- **Colors:** Deep walnut to deep black, or antique gold to bright gold
- **Opacity:** 20-40% overlay on images
- **Use:** Hero sections, featured content areas

#### Vignette Effect
- **Style:** Darkened edges, lighter center
- **Intensity:** Subtle, 20-30% darkening
- **Shape:** Radial or rectangular
- **Use:** Product photography, hero images

#### Depth & Shadows
- **Elevation:** Use 3 levels (low, medium, high)
- **Low:** 0 2px 4px rgba(0,0,0,0.2)
- **Medium:** 0 4px 12px rgba(0,0,0,0.3)
- **High:** 0 8px 24px rgba(0,0,0,0.4)

### Decorative Flourishes

#### Bauernmalerei-Style Ornaments
- **Characteristics:** Symmetrical floral/leaf designs
- **Color:** Antique gold with cream highlights
- **Size:** Small accents, not dominating
- **Placement:** Corner accents, section breaks, certificate borders

#### Butterfly Variations
- **Detailed:** Full butterfly for logo and primary placements
- **Simplified:** Silhouette version for patterns or watermarks
- **Abstract:** Geometric interpretation for modern applications
- **Opacity:** 5-10% for watermarks, 100% for features

#### Oak Leaf Accents
- **Single Leaf:** Small accent at line ends or corners
- **Branch:** Multiple leaves for larger spaces
- **Style:** Stylized, not photorealistic
- **Use:** Seasonal promotions (autumn), German heritage emphasis

### Texture & Finish

#### Print Finishes
- **Matte:** Primary finish for sophisticated feel
- **Soft-Touch:** Business cards and premium materials
- **Spot UV:** On logo or butterfly for subtle luxury
- **Foil:** Antique gold foil for premium applications

#### Digital Textures
- **Paper Texture:** Subtle grain for digital materials (5-10% opacity)
- **Fabric Texture:** Linen texture for warm, tactile feel
- **Noise:** Light grain/noise for depth (2-5% opacity)

## Video Content Guidelines

### Platform Specifications

#### Instagram Reels / TikTok
- **Aspect Ratio:** 9:16 vertical (1080x1920px)
- **Duration:** 15-30 seconds ideal, 60 seconds max
- **Style:** Authentic, unpolished charm with intentional warmth
- **Music:** Acoustic, folk, or classical German (royalty-free)
- **Text Overlay:** Sans-serif, cream (#faf8f3) or antique gold (#d4af37)

#### YouTube Shorts
- **Same as Reels/TikTok specs**
- **Focus:** Educational content about coffee or German traditions

#### Stories (Instagram/Facebook)
- **Duration:** 5-15 seconds per story
- **Quantity:** 3-5 stories per sequence
- **Interactive Elements:** Polls, questions in brand colors

### Content Themes

#### Daily Rituals
- Morning cart setup (time-lapse)
- First brew of the day (steam and golden light)
- 3 PM Kaffeezeit bell/chime
- Closing ritual (cleaning with care)

#### Process Shots
- Espresso extraction (macro lens)
- Milk steaming technique
- Latte art creation
- Hand-grinding beans (ASMR potential)

#### People & Moments
- Hands wrapped around warm cups
- Customer reactions (with permission)
- Team member spotlights
- Generational coffee moments (young and old together)

### Video Editing Guidelines
- **Transitions:** Gentle fades or cuts, no harsh effects
- **Color Grading:** Match photo warmth guidelines
- **Pace:** Relaxed, never rushed (honor the Kaffeezeit spirit)
- **Captions:** Always include for accessibility
- **Watermark:** Subtle butterfly icon in corner
